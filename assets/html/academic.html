Biomedical Engineering — Polytechnique Montréal

2013 – 2018

From 2013 to 2018, I studied Biomedical Engineering at École Polytechnique de Montréal. These were formative years in which I learned an enormous amount — both academically and professionally. There were highs and some very challenging lows, but it marked the true beginning of my engineering career.

What is a biomedical engineer? In the words of Polytechnique Montréal:

"The biomedical engineer applies the principles of engineering to the study, modification, and control of biological systems, as well as to the design and manufacture of products for monitoring physiological functions and assisting in patient diagnosis and treatment."

Notable Projects

Biomedical Engineering at Polytechnique involved a wide variety of hands-on projects, from designing a prototype biomedical instrument to collect and analyze physiological signals, to developing an iPhone application to assist HIV patients with medication adherence, to a biomechanics project measuring the velocity of a baseball swing. The projects were as varied as they were numerous.

A Non-Linear Path

My time at Polytechnique was anything but linear. Alternating constantly between co-op internships and coursework, I quickly realized this was less a purely academic journey and more a professional one. Plans changed frequently, and each co-op rotation brought a new environment, new challenges, and new skills that fed back into the classroom experience.

Outside the Classroom

Beyond coursework and internships, university was also a time of personal growth — building habits of independent learning, collaborating in multidisciplinary teams, and developing the adaptability that has defined my career since graduation.
